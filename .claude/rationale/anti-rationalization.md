# Anti-Rationalization Rationale

Why: `.claude/rules/anti-rationalization.md`

## Why Pre-Address Excuses

Rules are only as good as adherence under pressure. When tests fail, deadlines loom, or code is already written, the temptation is to rationalize shortcuts. The tables pre-address specific excuses so there's no judgment call.

## Why No Performative Agreement

"You're absolutely right!" and "Great point!" are sycophancy, not understanding. Fix the problem, describe the change, move on.

## Why 3-Fix Rule

Three failures suggest a wrong mental model. More attempts in the same direction waste time. Step back, reassess fundamentals, discuss with user.
